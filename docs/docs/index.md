# AutoNyan

**Drop a document into Google Drive, and AutoNyan files it away for you.**

AutoNyan watches a Google Drive folder, reads each document you add, sorts it
into the right category, and emails you a summary of what it did. You keep
working in Drive the way you already do — AutoNyan handles the tidying in the
background.

## What it does for you

- **Reads your documents** — text is extracted automatically from the files you
  drop in, including scans and photos of paper documents.
- **Sorts them into categories** — each document is classified and moved into
  the matching folder, so related papers end up together without manual filing.
- **Tells you what happened** — you receive a notification email summarizing
  which documents were processed and where they were filed.

## Who it's for

This site is for **people who use a running AutoNyan instance** — you add
documents to a shared Drive folder and rely on AutoNyan to organize them. You do
**not** need to deploy, operate, or understand the underlying cloud
infrastructure to follow these guides.

:::note

Building, deploying, or operating AutoNyan yourself? That is covered by the
[developer documentation](https://github.com/kkohtaka/AutoNyan#readme) in the
project repository, not this site.

:::
